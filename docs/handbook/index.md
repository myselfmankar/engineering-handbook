# The Engineering Handbook

This is not a static list of definitions. This is a **living repository** for engineering excellence — built to track my evolution from a consumer of technology to a distiller of complex distributed systems.

---

## 🏗️ Our Philosophy: "Distill to Master"

In an era of information overload, the greatest skill an engineer can have is the ability to **distill complexity**. 

The purpose of this handbook is to force a rigorous learning cycle:
1. **Active Research**: Moving beyond tutorials to academic papers and real engineering blogs.
2. **First-Principles Thinking**: Breaking every system down until the "Why" behind every trade-off is clear.
3. **Structured Documentation**: Re-explaining these concepts in my own words to ensure true mastery.

---

## 💻 Developing in Public

I chose to build this knowledge base in public for three main reasons:
- **Accountability**: Knowing others might read this forces a higher standard of accuracy and clarity.
- **Collaboration**: Great engineering is a team sport. I invite my peers to challenge my models and contribute their own insights.
- **Transparency**: I want my learning journey to be visible — documenting not just what I know, but how my understanding of systems matures over time.

---

## 📈 An Evolving Document

The content within these folders will grow, shift, and be refactored as my own technical depth increases. You aren't just reading a wiki; you're witnessing the **evolution of an engineer's mental model.**
